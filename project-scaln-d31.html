Project Detail

ScAlN d31 Characterization

This project focuses on the characterization of the d31 piezoelectric coefficient in scandium aluminum nitride thin films. The work is intended to provide a reliable experimental basis for understanding in-plane piezoelectric response and its relevance to electromechanical device performance.

By quantifying d31 behavior under controlled measurement conditions, the project supports both material validation and the design of actuators, resonators, and related thin-film piezoelectric structures.

Project Scope

The project covers thin-film sample preparation context, electrode or structure considerations, and measurement interpretation related to extracting an effective d31 response from ScAlN-based devices.

Current Focus

  • Building a consistent characterization procedure.
  • Comparing measured response across device conditions.
  • Establishing a reliable basis for performance benchmarking.

Expected Outcome

The expected outcome is a clear and reproducible characterization framework that can be used to assess ScAlN material quality and guide device design decisions for piezoelectric applications.
