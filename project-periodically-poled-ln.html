Project Detail

Periodically Poled LN for Acoustic Nonlinearity

This project investigates periodically poled lithium niobate structures as a platform for studying and exploiting acoustic nonlinearity. The central idea is to use engineered domain patterns to influence nonlinear acoustic interactions in a controlled and physically meaningful way.

The work is relevant to wave manipulation, nonlinear response engineering, and the development of advanced material-enabled acoustic devices with tailored behavior.

Project Scope

The project includes domain-engineered structure considerations, nonlinear acoustic response analysis, and interpretation of how periodic poling may affect wave propagation and interaction behavior.

Current Focus

  • Studying how periodic poling modifies acoustic response.
  • Connecting material structure to nonlinear wave behavior.
  • Exploring device opportunities enabled by tailored nonlinearity.

Expected Outcome

The expected outcome is a stronger understanding of how engineered ferroelectric domain structures can be used to control nonlinear acoustic effects and expand the design space of lithium niobate-based devices.
